A BRIEF HISTORY

The Uganda Martyrs were 45 men aged between 14 and 50, who had converted to christianity and as result of that were executed from 1885 to 1887 on orders by the kabaka (king) Mwanga ll of buganda kingdom now the republic of Uganda. Most of them were burned,some castrated, beheaded and dismembered.Saint Kizito omuto was the youngest at only fourteen years of age, he among others is notable.

AFTERMATH

Their execution was meant to serve as a warning to everyone in the Buganda kingdom what would happen if they fell short of their alleigance to the kabaka (king). However it instead exasperated the interest and growth of christianity in Uganda, even to day every 3rd of June is a public holiday in Uganda to commemorate the faith and bravery of the Uganda Martyrs, lest they are forgotten.

COMMEMORATION

People from all walks of life and from all over the world make pilgrimage to Uganda Martyrs shrine at Namugongo in Uganda to pay tribute and pray to the Martyrs as they were canonised by Pope Paul VI in 1964 as Saints and their remeberance day 3rd june is part of the Roman calender. Pope Francis is one of the notable pilgrims who visited the Uganda Martyrs shrine in 2015.
